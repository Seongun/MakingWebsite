What is a Makerspace?

A makerspace, as the word itself implies, is a space set up with the intention to make, to build {insert object}.



However, in its essence, makerspaces denote more than that. They represent a non-formal education tool through the methods it makes use of, it denotes a collaborative and learning-conducive environment with a less restrictive curriculum than its traditional counterpart hence leaving more room for creativity and innovation.

“Makerspaces represent the democratization of design, engineering, fabrication, and education.” (spaces.makerspace)

"A makerspace is a place where students can gather to create, invent, tinker, explore and discover using a variety of tools and materials." (renovatedlearning)

"There are no rules here — we're trying to accomplish something." Thomas Edison

What is a Makerspace?

As we have seen, the definition of makerspace is very broad. Makerspaces vary from family garages to sustainable businesses. However, in the context of education we believe that makerspaces should share the following properties:

Project driven - critical thinking skills, solving real life problems, preparing for maker faire or science fair rather than pure tool training.

Inclusive - there is no place for discrimination (do not confuse with constructive criticism) in the makerspace, everyone is welcome, if students are willing to join the makerspace they should be able to do so at no cost. If students are willing to obey established rules of the space (safety related rules, rules against stealing, etc) they should be able to continue being involved with the space at no cost

Multidisciplinary - makerspaces are not only about woodworking, electronics and soldering. Anything that involves making, such as crafts, sewing, egg painting, etc should be welcome in the makerspace

Fostering Apprenticeship - makerspace should encourage apprenticeship and mentorship. If someone is developing a project and is willing to use the tool that they haven’t used before, they should be able to receive help or observe someone who is using this tool for their own project.
