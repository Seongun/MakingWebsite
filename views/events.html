Workshops

Workshops can be a great way to introduce students and beginners to the tools in the makerspace.

However, the focus of a workshop should not simply be about learning how to use tools. Instead, a good workshop should aim to have students produce something of value. They can either take their project home, display it in the makerspace, or take photos and videos of the project and perhaps post these in a blog or personal website.

Centering workshops around a project to be build rather than about a tool to be studied is crucial. This is because it helps students understand how the tool is used in the real-world and emphasizes the principles of project-based learning. Building something tangible in a workshop will also give students the confidence to embark on further, more challenging projects in the future.

Events

Events can be a great way to create some excitement about the makerspace and get people involved with the space!

Events can also help beginners understand what a makerspace is all about. Programs should try to cater to a diverse range of visitors (students of different ages, parents, teachers and professionals) and should feature student work and projects prominently.
