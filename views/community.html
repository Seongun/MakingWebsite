Community Building

Community Building

The driving factor of a makerspace is a community around it. The best-equipped maker space that is not accessible has no potential to enforce learning and benefit the community. However, a small community-driven space with really limited resources but motivated participants can achieve amazing results.

In the beginning, it might be difficult to attract people and inform them about the space. That is why it important to find a core group of people, who can are motivated and are willing to invest their time into community building in the space. We compile several resources that could be a starting point for building the community around the space:

  • Workshops
  • Events
  • Programs

One of the programs we propose is titled Makerstars.

Maker Stars

We mostly looked at building Makerspace at school, and we found that it is absolutely crucial to have student-led initiatives. To encourage and train a group of motivated students we propose offering positions of Makerstarts. Maker stars are encouraged to spend lots of time in the space working on their projects and helping other. They could also hold lab hours formally, where they teach and help others.

Makerstars are most likely to be students, who are excited, motivated, reliable and dedicated. Makerstars should be familiar with the mission of the makerspace. They should know how to use most of the tools in the space and most importantly, they should enjoy being in the space. Appointing Makerstars on this position should give them a sense of ownership and fulfillment, which would be a motivating factor for them to perform their best, grow personally and professionally and meet people.
